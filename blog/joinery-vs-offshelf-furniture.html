Joinery vs. off-the-shelf furniture — when custom saves money

6 min read · By The Walls Team · April 2025

The most common objection we hear from clients considering custom joinery is the upfront cost. And it's valid — a custom built-in wardrobe costs more than a flatpack alternative. But the comparison isn't as simple as AED per square metre, and in most Dubai homes, custom joinery is the financially smarter choice over a 5–10 year horizon.

Here's how we think about it — and where the calculation doesn't hold.

Where custom joinery wins

Dubai apartments and villas often have unusual ceiling heights, awkward alcoves, and non-standard room shapes. Off-the-shelf furniture is designed for the median European or American home — not a 3.2-metre ceiling in JBR or an irregular walk-in wardrobe in a Palm villa.

When furniture doesn't fit the space precisely, you get gaps, filler panels, and visual clutter. Custom joinery fills every millimetre. That precision isn't just aesthetic — it maximises storage volume by 30–50% compared to equivalent off-the-shelf pieces in the same floor area.

The durability gap

Premium off-the-shelf brands (Poliform, Rimadesio, Boffi) use good materials, but even they rely on modular panel sizes and standardised hardware. Our bespoke joinery uses E1-grade boards or better, full-extension soft-close hardware, and finishes applied in controlled workshop conditions. We see 15–20 year lifespans on correctly specified custom joinery. Most branded flatpack systems start showing wear at 7–10 years in UAE humidity.

The real cost comparison

Let's use a master bedroom wardrobe as an example. A premium sliding-door wardrobe system from a European brand, installed, typically costs AED 18,000–28,000 for a 4-metre run in Dubai. Our equivalent custom piece — same footprint, full-height to ceiling, soft-close internals, lacquer finish — runs AED 22,000–32,000.

The custom option is 15–20% more expensive upfront. But it uses every centimetre of your ceiling height (gaining 30–40% more hanging and shelving space), fits the exact wall dimensions with no filler panels, and can be configured with exactly the internal layout you need rather than a preset modular option.

Amortised over 10 years, the custom piece works out to less per year of usable life — and it stays with the property, adding to resale value in a way that branded flatpack doesn't.

When off-the-shelf makes more sense

We're not evangelical about custom joinery. There are situations where we'll tell clients to go to a furniture retailer instead:

  • Short-term rentals. If you're furnishing an investment property for 2-year rental cycles, standard furniture is easier to replace and tenant-neutral.
  • Fully furnished units. If the developer package already includes built-ins of reasonable quality, replacing them for the sake of custom is waste.
  • Tight timelines. Custom joinery requires 4–8 weeks in fabrication. If you need furniture in 2 weeks, a good showroom will serve you better.
  • Budget-sensitive secondary rooms. Guest bedrooms and storage rooms rarely justify premium custom joinery — a well-specified off-the-shelf solution works perfectly.

Our approach: selective custom

On most projects, we recommend custom joinery for the spaces where it delivers maximum value: master bedroom wardrobes, kitchen cabinetry, living room wall units and TV panels, and entrance/foyer joinery. For children's rooms and utility spaces, we often specify a commercial-grade system that performs well at lower cost.

The decision should follow the space, not a blanket policy. If you'd like to talk through which areas of your project justify custom and which don't, book a consultation with us — we'll give you an honest assessment, not a sales pitch.
