How long does a villa fit-out take in Dubai?

8 min read · By The Walls Team · April 2025

One of the first questions clients ask us is: "How long will this actually take?" It's a fair question — and one that deserves a straight answer rather than a vague "it depends."

For a full villa fit-out in Dubai — covering joinery, solid surfaces, aluminum works, ceilings, and finishes — the realistic range is 8 to 16 weeks from design sign-off to final handover. Below, we break down every phase so you can plan your move-in date with confidence.

Phase 1: Design & documentation (2–4 weeks)

Before a single panel gets cut, we need approved drawings. This phase covers space planning, material selection, shop drawings for custom joinery, and coordination with your main contractor if works are concurrent.

If you already have a complete interior design package from an architect, this phase can compress to 1–2 weeks. If we're designing from scratch, allow the full 4 weeks. Material selection — especially for solid surfaces like Corian and quartz countertops — can extend timelines if special colours need to be sourced.

Phase 2: Workshop fabrication (4–8 weeks)

This is where most of the time goes. Custom joinery — wardrobes, kitchen cabinets, TV units, wall panels, office desks — is fabricated in our workshop while site preparation happens in parallel.

A standard 4-bedroom villa with full joinery across all rooms typically takes 5–6 weeks in fabrication. A lighter scope (living room and master suite only) can be done in 3–4 weeks. Factors that slow fabrication include unusual materials, custom hardware orders, and lacquer finishes that require multiple cure cycles.

What we fabricate off-site

  • All wardrobe and cabinet carcasses and doors
  • Kitchen modules and drawer systems
  • TV units, display shelving, and feature wall panels
  • Solid surface countertops (templated after base units are installed)
  • Aluminum window frames, railings, and decorative screens

Phase 3: On-site installation (2–5 weeks)

Installation is faster than fabrication, but it requires a clean, coordinated site. Joinery goes in first, followed by solid surface templating and fabrication, then aluminum works, and finally snagging.

For a typical Palm Jumeirah villa — 4 bedrooms, open-plan living, full kitchen — we budget 3 weeks on-site. Concurrent trades (tiling, painting, electrical) can slow progress if scheduling isn't tight. We always recommend a dedicated fit-out period rather than working alongside other contractors.

Phase 4: Finishing & handover (3–5 days)

The final phase covers touch-ups, hardware polishing, door adjustments, deep cleaning, and the official snag walk. We don't hand over until every drawer slides smoothly and every surface is flawless. For a full villa, this typically takes 3 to 5 days.

Common causes of delays

  • Late material approvals. If clients take 2–3 weeks to confirm finishes, the fabrication clock doesn't start.
  • Site not ready. Flooring and ceiling work must be complete before joinery installation begins.
  • Design changes mid-build. Changes after fabrication starts can set the schedule back 2–4 weeks.
  • Material lead times. Specialty hardware (European fittings, custom handles) can take 3–6 weeks to arrive in Dubai.

Our honest recommendation

If you need to move in by a specific date, work backwards from that date and add 2 weeks as a buffer. Start the design phase before your lease ends or handover date arrives — the worst-case scenario is finishing 2 weeks early, which gives you time to furnish properly.

We've completed full villa fit-outs in 7 weeks when the design package was locked before we started. We've also seen projects run 5 months when changes kept accumulating. The single biggest factor is design discipline — every change after fabrication starts costs time and money.
