Office fit-out checklist for DIFC and DMCC tenants

9 min read · The Walls Editorial · April 2025

DIFC and DMCC are Dubai's two most prominent commercial free zones — and both have approval and compliance requirements that regularly catch first-time tenants off guard. Starting a fit-out without understanding the process leads to stop-work orders, costly rework, and delayed move-in dates.

This checklist covers everything you need before breaking ground. We've completed multiple office fit-outs in DIFC and can confirm that these requirements are enforced.

Before you sign the lease

Before committing to a unit, confirm the following with the landlord or building management:

  • The unit's base fit-out condition (shell and core, Cat A, or Cat B) — this determines how much work is required
  • Whether the base MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) serves the unit or whether new connections are needed
  • Any existing partitioning that cannot be removed (structural or fire-rated walls)
  • Noise restrictions during fit-out works (most DIFC towers restrict noisy works to outside business hours)
  • Building management's approved contractor list — some buildings require pre-qualification

DIFC-specific requirements

DIFC Facilities Management (DIFCFM) has a structured NOC (No Objection Certificate) process for all fit-out works:

  • Contractor pre-qualification. Your fit-out contractor must be registered with DIFCFM. First-time contractors go through a qualification review (allow 2–3 weeks). We are pre-qualified.
  • Design approval. Architectural and MEP drawings must be submitted to and approved by DIFCFM before works begin. Allow 3–5 business days for routine submissions; longer if revisions are required.
  • Trade permits. Individual trade permits (civil, MEP, low-voltage) must be obtained for each scope of works. These are issued per contractor, not per project.
  • Working hours. Noisy works (drilling, chipping, core drilling) are restricted to 7pm–7am on weekdays and full weekends. This significantly extends timelines for projects with heavy civil works.
  • Materials storage. DIFC does not permit materials to be stored in common areas or lobbies. Your contractor must sequence deliveries carefully.

DMCC-specific requirements

DMCC (Jumeirah Lakes Towers) operates through the JLT Owners' Association and individual building management offices. Requirements vary more between buildings than in DIFC, but common requirements include:

  • NOC from building management. Issued per project, typically within 3–5 business days of complete submission
  • Contractor insurance. Public liability of AED 5–10 million, depending on building requirements
  • Lift protection. All goods lifts must be protected with approved padding during fit-out works
  • Waste management. Debris removal must be via approved skip contractors — not through residential waste facilities
  • Fire barrier compliance. Any penetration through fire-rated walls or floors must be reinstated to the original fire rating with approved materials, signed off by a qualified fire engineer

The fit-out process: step by step

Step 1 — Space planning and concept design (Week 1–2)

Finalise the layout: open plan vs. private offices, meeting room count, pantry location, server room if required. This determines the entire MEP requirement.

Step 2 — Shop drawing preparation (Week 2–4)

Prepare full architectural, joinery, and MEP drawings for submission. Include reflected ceiling plans, partition layouts, and joinery elevations.

Step 3 — Submit for DIFC/DMCC approval (Week 4–6)

Submit the full drawing package to the relevant authority. Follow up proactively — approvals can stall for weeks if comments are not addressed promptly.

Step 4 — Fabrication (Week 4–8, concurrent with approval)

Custom joinery fabrication can begin once designs are locked, even before site approval arrives. This keeps the schedule tight.

Step 5 — Site works (Week 6–12)

Partitioning, raised access flooring, ceiling grid, MEP first fix, joinery installation, MEP second fix, decorating, and AV/IT rough-in. For a 500–800 sqm DIFC office, allow 6–8 weeks on site.

Step 6 — Fit-out completion inspection (Week 12–13)

DIFCFM or building management will conduct a completion inspection before issuing the occupation permit. Address any outstanding items before requesting inspection — failed inspections cause delays of 5–10 business days.

Typical budget ranges (DIFC/DMCC, 2025)

  • Standard office fit-out (open plan, modest joinery): AED 800–1,100/sqm
  • Premium office fit-out (custom joinery, executive suites, feature reception): AED 1,500–2,500/sqm
  • Executive boutique office (full bespoke joinery, feature walls, premium finishes): AED 2,500–4,000/sqm

These ranges include design, permits, and installation but exclude furniture, AV/IT infrastructure, and signage.

One mistake to avoid

The most common error we see is clients engaging a contractor before the lease is signed and approval requirements are understood. The contractor quotes a price, the client signs, and then the approval process reveals requirements that weren't scoped — extra fire doors, MEP upgrades, or specialist subcontractors mandated by the building.

Always clarify the full compliance requirement before requesting quotes. A good contractor will help you scope this correctly before committing to a price.
