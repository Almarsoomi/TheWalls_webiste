What to ask before hiring a fit-out contractor in the UAE

5 min read · By The Walls Team · April 2025

We've taken over several projects mid-build where the original contractor failed — ran out of cash, disappeared, or delivered work that required complete demolition and restart. In every case, the warning signs were there before the contract was signed. The client just didn't know which questions to ask.

Here are the questions we'd ask if we were sitting across the table from a fit-out contractor — including us.

Licensing and insurance

This is non-negotiable. Any legitimate fit-out contractor in Dubai should hold:

  • A valid Dubai Economic Department (DED) trade licence for fit-out and interior decoration
  • Public liability insurance (minimum AED 5 million coverage for residential projects)
  • A valid NOC or approval to work in your specific building or community (for master-developed communities like Emaar, Nakheel, or DIFC)

Ask to see the licence and insurance certificate before signing. A contractor who hesitates to provide these doesn't have them.

Who will actually be on site?

Many Dubai fit-out companies win the contract, then subcontract the work to cheaper labour. Ask directly: who fabricates your joinery? Is it your workshop or a third party? Who is the site supervisor and will they be present every day?

The answer matters because quality control lives with the person on site, not the salesperson in the showroom. We run our own workshop and assign a dedicated site supervisor to every project who reports directly to the project architect.

Can you see completed work in person?

Portfolio images can be selective and retouched. Ask to visit a completed project — ideally one that is 2–3 years old, so you can see how the work has held up. Pay attention to drawer alignment, edge banding quality, finish consistency, and whether silicone joints have yellowed.

A reputable contractor will have no hesitation giving you references and arranging visits. If they only offer to show you renders or photos, that's a red flag.

What are your payment terms?

Standard payment structure for UAE fit-out projects:

  • 25–30% deposit at contract signing (covers materials procurement)
  • 30–35% at start of installation
  • 25–30% at practical completion
  • 10% retention held for 3–6 months after handover

Any contractor asking for 50%+ upfront should be treated with caution. It can indicate cash flow problems. The retention clause is your leverage to ensure snagging items are completed — never waive it.

What warranty do you offer?

In the UAE, there is no mandatory contractor warranty period for fit-out works (unlike in some European markets). However, a confident contractor will offer:

  • Minimum 1-year workmanship warranty on joinery installation
  • Manufacturer warranty passed through on hardware (typically 2–5 years for Blum, Häfele, and similar brands)
  • Written commitment to respond to warranty calls within 48 hours

We offer a 1-year full workmanship warranty and have a dedicated snagging team that handles post-handover issues within 48 hours.

How do you handle design changes?

Changes happen on every project. The question is how they're priced and documented. A professional contractor will issue a written variation order for any change, with the cost impact clearly stated, before proceeding. Informal agreements ("we'll sort it out at the end") almost always lead to disputes.

Ask to see an example variation order form. If they don't have one, they're managing changes informally — which is how budgets spiral.

One last question

Ask the contractor: "What is the biggest problem you've encountered on a project, and how did you resolve it?" The answer tells you more than any portfolio. Honest contractors have good stories about problems they solved. Contractors who claim everything always goes smoothly have either never done complex work or aren't being straight with you.
